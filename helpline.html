F.A.Q

  • Basic Treatment of Skin Cancer?

    Chemotherapy is a cancer treatment that uses drugs to stop the growth of cancer cells, either by killing the cells or by stopping them from dividing. Chemotherapy for basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma of the skin, and actinic keratosis is usually topical (applied to the skin in a cream or lotion).

  • What kind of food shouls be consumed to fight against Skin Cancer?

    Carrots and leafy greens like kale and spinach are great beta carotene-packed additions to your meals, even breakfast smoothies. In particular, leafy greens are high in the antioxidants lutein and zeaxanthin. These have been found to protect against wrinkling, sun damage, and even skin cancer.

  • How is skin cancer prevented?

    1. Stay in the shade, especially during midday hours.
    2. Wear clothing that covers your arms and legs.
    3. Wear a hat with a wide brim to shade your face, head, ears, and neck.
    4. Wear sunglasses that wrap around and block both UVA and UVB rays.
