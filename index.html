What is Skin Cancer?

Skin Cancer is the abnormal growth of the skin cells. There are two main categories of Skin Cancer – "Keratinocyte Carcinoma"Basal cell carcinoma and squamous cell carcinoma are generally grouped together and called “Keratinocyte Carcinoma,” because they begin in a type of skin cell called a keratinocyte. They are also called “non-melanoma skin cancer” to distinguish them from melanoma. and "Melanoma"Melanoma, the most serious type of skin cancer, develops in the cells (melanocytes) that produce Melanin (the pigment which gives the skin color).
